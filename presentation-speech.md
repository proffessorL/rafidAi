### Feature 1: Smart Mistake Analysis (1.5 minutes)

> "When a student gets a quiz question wrong, most platforms show a red mark and move on. RESNOR does something fundamentally different.
>
> Our system actually **analyzes the mistake**. It looks at what the student answered, what the correct answer was, and uses AI to figure out *why* they got it wrong. Was it a misunderstanding of the concept? A careless error? A logic gap? The system classifies the mistake into a specific category and then generates a **personalized explanation** — not a generic answer key, but an explanation written specifically for that student's wrong answer.
>
> And here's what makes it legit: every mistake is **tracked over time**. If the same student keeps making the same type of error across different quizzes, the system detects that pattern. It builds a profile of their weak areas automatically. Teachers can see this data and know exactly where to help. This isn't just AI for show — it's AI that actually **learns from every wrong answer** and helps students improve."

---

### Feature 2: Burnout Prediction Using Machine Learning (1.5 minutes)

> "Student burnout is a real problem. Long study hours, low mood, late nights, dropping quiz scores — these patterns often show up *before* a student actually burns out. But most students don't notice the pattern until it's too late.
>
> RESNOR uses a **real machine learning algorithm** to detect these patterns early. Every day, the system looks at five simple things: how many hours you studied, your mood, whether you've been studying late at night, and whether your quiz scores are dropping. It combines these into a **daily profile**.
>
> Then it compares today's profile against your own past data. If today looks similar to previous days when you were burned out, the system warns you. Think of it like a health tracker — but for your academic well-being.
>
> What makes this legit is that the system **gets more accurate the more you use it**. Every day you check in, it has more data to compare against. It's personalized — not a generic formula, but a model built on **your own patterns**. And it runs on a real KNN algorithm, not a mocked calculation."

---

### Feature 3: Smart Engagement Tracker (1.5 minutes)

> "The third feature is something most EdTech platforms completely miss. It's not enough to know *that* a student is studying — you need to know *how* they're studying. Are they actually learning, or just clicking around?
>
> RESNOR's Engagement Tracker monitors every interaction a student makes across the platform. It tracks time spent on each feature — the Quiz Generator, the AI Tutor, Study Notes, the Discussion Forum, everything. And here's the smart part: it classifies every session as **active** or **passive** based on real behavior. If a student is on a page but barely interacting or the tab isn't focused, that's marked as passive time. If they're answering questions, writing notes, solving problems — that's active learning time.
>
> The system shows a **daily goal ring** — like a fitness tracker, but for studying. It breaks down time by feature so students can see where their hours actually go. Are they spending 2 hours on the AI Tutor but only 20 minutes actively engaged? The system flags that as a **passive sink** and tells them to switch to more interactive activities.
>
> There's also a **7-day trend chart** that shows active versus passive time day by day, so students can see patterns: 'I was really engaged on Monday but just browsing on Wednesday. What changed?'
>
> And on top of all this, an **AI coach** analyzes the engagement patterns and gives personalized suggestions — like 'You spent 40 minutes on the Discussion Forum today with low interaction. Try writing a post instead of just reading.' or 'Great focus today — 80% of your time was active learning.'
>
> What makes this legit is that it's all based on **real interaction data**, not screen time estimates. The system tracks actual clicks, tab focus, session activity. It knows the difference between watching and doing — and that's the difference between passive scrolling and real learning."
